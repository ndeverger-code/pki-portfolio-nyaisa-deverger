Step 3 — Validate the Chain (and Observe the Failure)
------------------------------------------------------
Q: What error did openssl verify leaf_cert.pem produce before you added the intermediate?
A: error 20 at 0 depth lookup: unable to get local issuer certificate — OpenSSL could not find the intermediate CA needed to verify the leaf.

Q: After adding the intermediate with -untrusted, did verification succeed?
A: Yes — "leaf_cert.pem: OK"

Q: What does the -untrusted flag mean in this context? Why is the intermediate "untrusted"?
A: The -untrusted flag tells OpenSSL to use the provided certificate as a potential chain-building certificate, but not to trust it on its own. The intermediate is called "untrusted" because it is not in the system's trust store — it still needs to chain up to a trusted root CA to be valid. OpenSSL will use it to build the path from leaf to root, but the root must be trusted independently.

Step 4 — Check Revocation and Trust
-------------------------------------
Q: Once the chain is repaired, is the root CA trusted by your system?
A: Yes — when the intermediate (R13) was provided locally with -untrusted, openssl verify returned "OK," meaning the root CA (ISRG Root X1 / Let's Encrypt) is in the system trust store. The server still returns verify code 21 because it does not send the intermediate — that is a server configuration problem, not a trust store problem.

Q: Is there any indication of a revocation issue?
A: No — this is purely a missing intermediate chain issue. There is no indication of certificate revocation.
